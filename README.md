# MO-GSR-BS
MO-GSR-BS: A Multi-Objective Graph-Relational and Evolutionary Approach for Hyperspectral Band Selection

Hyperspectral image (HSI) analysis requires effective band selection (BS) techniques to mitigate high-dimensional redundancy while enhancing classification accuracy. While recent similarity-based ranking approaches, such as SR-NMI-VI, provide a robust foundation by leveraging informational attributes, they often optimize for a single objective, potentially overlooking the critical trade-off between subset sparsity and discriminative performance. This paper proposes a Multi-Objective Graph-Similarity-Reinforced Band Selection (MO-GSR-BS) framework. The approach first models the HSI spectral space as a weighted relational graph, where edge weights are derived from a hybrid of Normalized Mutual Information (NMI) and Structural Similarity (SSIM) to capture both informational and spatial-spectral dependencies. Spectral bands are subsequently prioritized using a weighted reach metric, defined as the degree centrality of each node, to identify the primary informational hubs within the spectral manifold. Subsequently, a Non-dominated Sorting Genetic Algorithm II (NSGA-II) is employed to simultaneously optimize two conflicting objectives: maximizing classification accuracy and minimizing the number of selected bands. Experimental validation on the Oil Spill and WHU-Hi Longkou datasets demonstrates that the proposed MO-GSR-BS identifies a Pareto-optimal front of band subsets, achieving a 50-60\% reduction in the number of bands while improving classification accuracy by 1\%. In particular, on the WHU-Hi Longkou dataset, the framework identified a subset of only 15 bands that achieved an overall accuracy of 94.15\%, significantly outperforming state-of-the-art wrappers. The results underscore the effectiveness of graph-relational modeling and multi-objective optimization in identifying compact, high-performing band subsets for resource-constrained HSI applications.
